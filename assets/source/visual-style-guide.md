# Visual style guide

## Direction

Radical humanism.

The site should feel bold, direct, warm, slightly strange, and unmistakably made by a real person. It should not look like a recruiter, a SaaS product, a polished personal-brand template, or a generic white-coded landing page.

The balance:

- confident, not self-important
- funny, not jokey
- visually bold, not chaotic
- personal, not sentimental
- selective, not elitist

## Core visual idea

The design is built around continuous compositions rather than boxed sections.

The strongest example is the hero-to-About transition:

- the hero sits on a saturated red field
- the About section sits on warm cream
- Matei’s full-body Chewbacca photo crosses the boundary
- most of the body remains on red
- one foot lands visibly on the cream section below

This overlap is essential. It makes the page feel like one composition rather than stacked rectangles.

## Clean About section

The About section should remain intentionally sparse.

Do not place a crowd image, team image, or any secondary photo in this section.

The three achievement statements should have room to breathe and should not compete with another visual element.

Use empty space as part of the design.

## Typography

### Display

Use a condensed, high-impact sans serif for headings.

Recommended:

- Bebas Neue
- Oswald
- Anton
- Archivo Narrow

The headline should feel editorial and poster-like.

### Body

Use Inter or another highly legible neutral sans serif.

Increase spacing between paragraphs, especially in the hero and About copy.

### Handwritten accents

Use handwriting only for personality and emphasis:

- `(weird)`
- `this was taken before AI`
- `(for free)`
- small underlines, arrows, and marks

`(weird)` must appear before `People`, so the headline reads:

> (weird) People make great companies.

It should feel added by hand after the main headline was printed.

## Color palette

- Signal red: `#D6452D`
- Warm cream: `#EFE7D4`
- Near black: `#111111`
- Charcoal: `#1A1A1A`
- Muted grey: `#7A7A7A`

## Texture

Use subtle, restrained texture:

- paper grain
- faint ink variation
- rough underlines
- occasional tape
- slightly imperfect arrows

Avoid turning the page into a scrapbook.

## Imagery

Primary image:

- use the supplied original Chewbacca photo
- preserve the complete body
- preserve both arms, both hands, and both feet
- do not replace it with an AI-generated likeness
- do not crop the photo at the red-to-cream boundary
- let the body overlap naturally into the About section

Secondary imagery:

- one taped black-and-white landscape image may be used in the black form section
- no crowd image
- no generic startup-team imagery
- no stock portraits

## Section structure

1. Hero
2. About, briefly
3. How it works
4. Form
5. Minimal footer

No navigation menu.

The header contains only:

> Matei Psatta

## Desktop layout

- asymmetric hero
- headline left
- portrait middle-right
- handwritten annotation near portrait
- About section in three columns
- generous empty space
- How It Works in four columns
- black form section

## Mobile layout

- `(weird)` above `People`
- portrait placed beside or below the headline
- keep a small overlap into the cream About section
- About statements stack vertically
- How It Works becomes a vertical list
- form fields are full width

## Interaction

Keep interaction minimal:

- accessible talent/founder tabs
- subtle underline or button movement
- optional very light parallax on the portrait
- no ornamental animation
- respect `prefers-reduced-motion`

## Copy tone

The tone is factual, lightly self-aware, and dismissive of résumé theatre.

Achievements should land quickly:

- strong factual line
- short human aside
- move on

Examples:

- `Across Europe and the US.`
- `Long story.`
- `Fortunately.`
